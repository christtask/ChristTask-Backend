What are the main arguments against the Trinity?

Claim: The Trinity is a Contradiction

The Trinity doctrine is often misunderstood as claiming three gods, but it actually teaches one God in three distinct persons. This is a mystery of faith, not a contradiction.

  • Biblical Evidence: Matthew 28:19 shows the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as distinct yet unified
  • Historical Context: Early Church Fathers developed this doctrine to preserve both monotheism and Christ's divinity
  • Logical Consistency: The Trinity maintains God's unity while explaining His relational nature

The Trinity is the best explanation for how God can be both one and relational, as revealed in Scripture.

How do you respond to claims that the Bible has contradictions?

Claim: The Bible Has Contradictions

The alleged contradictions in the Bible are frequently based on surface-level readings or misunderstandings of context. Scholars have explained most supposed discrepancies through careful analysis.

  • Historical Context: Many apparent contradictions resolve when we understand the cultural and historical background
  • Original Languages: Translation nuances can create apparent conflicts that don't exist in the original text
  • Literary Genres: Different books use different writing styles and purposes
  • Progressive Revelation: God's revelation unfolds progressively throughout Scripture

When studied carefully with proper hermeneutics, the Bible demonstrates remarkable internal consistency.
